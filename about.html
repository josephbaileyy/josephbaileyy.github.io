Research

Neutrino cross-section unfolding with machine learning

Implementing and evaluating OmniFold, an unbinned ML unfolding framework, on MINERvA neutrino-scattering data — benchmarking it against iterative Bayesian unfolding through closure tests, generator stress tests, bootstrap resampling, and bias–variance analysis. The question: can high-dimensional unbinned unfolding reduce structural model bias in the few-GeV regime? Toward an honors thesis and manuscript.

Prof. Benjamin Nachman, Stanford Physics · 2026–present · 2026 Stanford Major Grant

Collider machine learning — SLAC

On a four-person team proposing a γγ collider, using Delphes, ROOT, and Unix HPC to simulate and analyze terabytes of collider data, applying ML to study pileup effects on di-Higgs detection.

SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory · 2024–present

Gaussian-process bump hunting — Jefferson Lab

Gaussian-process regression to search for excesses from the hypothetical X17 particle in collider data. Presented at the APS Far West Section meeting, UC Santa Cruz.

Jefferson Lab · 2025

AM CVn time-series photometry

Physically-motivated photometry of the ultracompact binary AM CVn: reduced 140 V-band CCD images and ran AAVSO differential photometry on 138 exposures, recovering variability consistent with the known 1051-second positive-superhump family.

PHYSICS 100: Observational Astrophysics · Spring 2026
