Research

Neutrino cross-section unfolding with machine learning

Implementing and evaluating OmniFold, an unbinned ML unfolding framework, on MINERvA neutrino-scattering data. I benchmark it against iterative Bayesian unfolding through closure tests, generator stress tests, bootstrap resampling, and bias–variance analysis, asking whether high-dimensional unbinned unfolding can reduce structural model bias in the few-GeV regime. Paper (PDF)

Prof. Benjamin Nachman, Stanford Physics · 2026–present · 2026 Stanford Major Grant

Collider machine learning — SLAC

On a four-person team proposing a γγ collider, using Delphes, ROOT, and Unix HPC to simulate and analyze terabytes of collider data and applying ML to study pileup effects on di-Higgs detection. Poster (PDF)

SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory · 2024–present

Gaussian-process bump hunting — Jefferson Lab

Used Gaussian-process regression to search for excesses from the hypothetical X17 particle in collider data and presented the work at the APS Far West Section meeting at UC Santa Cruz. APS talk (PDF)
